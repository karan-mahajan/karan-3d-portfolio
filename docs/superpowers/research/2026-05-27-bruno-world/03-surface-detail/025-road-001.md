## Technique / recipe

The "curve as source-of-truth + baked mesh segments as runtime output" pattern:

- **The curve is the authoring spline**, `BézierCircle.001`, a 27-control-point bezier covering the road's full sweep.
- **The 12 `Cube.*` meshes are the baked geometry** — likely produced by a Geometry-Nodes modifier that extruded the spline with a road-cross-section profile, then baked into segmented mesh chunks for runtime export.
- **All 12 mesh objects share an origin `(-1.731, -15.725, 1)`**: when baking the curve-driven output, Bruno used a single anchor object and let the per-segment mesh verts carry the world-space displacement. The runtime sees 12 sub-meshes all relative to the same origin.
- **`refRoad`** at a different location is the **reference / non-baked variant** — maybe a previous iteration or a template mesh. The "ref" prefix is a strong convention in Bruno's pipeline (also seen in `refBowlingPin`, `bumpersRefrenceMesh` — Bruno spells it "refrence" sometimes, but here he spells it "ref...").
- **`curveRoad` is hidden everywhere** (viewport/render/select) — confirms it's an authoring asset that should not be visible at runtime. The runtime exports the 12 baked meshes only; the curve is .blend-side history.
- **Slight Z lift (`+1.0`)** on the 12 road segments suggests the road sits 1 m above terrain at this anchor. Compare to slabs/bricks which usually sit `+0.01-0.02 m` above terrain — 1 m is a "lifted road" or maybe a stage / loading-zone.
